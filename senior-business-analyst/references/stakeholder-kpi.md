# Stakeholder Mapping, KPIs & Business Case Playbook

How to identify stakeholders, define success metrics, and build a business case. Load
this for "stakeholder map", "KPIs / success metrics", "business case".

## Stakeholder mapping

List everyone affected by or influencing the initiative, then classify them.

- **Power/Interest grid** — place each on high/low power × high/low interest:
  - High power + high interest → **manage closely** (key decision-makers)
  - High power + low interest → **keep satisfied**
  - Low power + high interest → **keep informed**
  - Low power + low interest → **monitor**

| Stakeholder | Role | Power | Interest | Needs / concern | Engagement |
|-------------|------|-------|----------|-----------------|------------|
| Payments lead | Sponsor | High | High | Digital txn growth | Manage closely |
| Ops team | User | Low | High | Less manual work | Keep informed |

- Note decision-makers (who signs off), contributors (who provides input), and the
  RACI (Responsible/Accountable/Consulted/Informed) for key decisions.
- Surface conflicting interests early and plan how to reconcile them.

## KPIs / success metrics

- Tie every goal to a metric. Make each metric **measurable** with a **baseline** and
  a **target** and a **timeframe**.
- Use leading (predictive, e.g. activation rate) and lagging (outcome, e.g. revenue)
  indicators. Avoid vanity metrics; pick ones tied to the business outcome.
- Define the metric precisely: formula, data source, segment, and how/when measured.

| KPI | Definition | Baseline | Target | Timeframe | Source |
|-----|------------|----------|--------|-----------|--------|
| Digital payment share | digital txns ÷ total txns | 42% | 60% | Q+1 | transaction-service data |

- Apply a guardrail metric (something that must *not* get worse, e.g. payment failure
  rate) so a win in one area doesn't quietly hurt another.

## Business case

- **Problem & opportunity** — the cost of today, the size of the prize.
- **Options** — including "do nothing"; pros/cons of each.
- **Benefits** — quantified where possible (revenue, cost saved, risk reduced,
  experience). Separate one-off vs recurring.
- **Costs** — build effort, run/ops cost, change-management.
- **Value & payback** — rough ROI / payback period; key assumptions and sensitivities.
- **Risks** — and mitigations.
- **Recommendation** — the option you recommend and why.

Keep it honest: state assumptions, give ranges not false precision, and make the
recommendation and the decision being asked for explicit.

## Quality bar

- All stakeholders identified and classified; decision-makers and RACI clear.
- Each goal has a precise, measured KPI with baseline + target + guardrail.
- Business case covers options (incl. do-nothing), quantified benefits/costs, risks,
  and a clear recommendation with stated assumptions.
